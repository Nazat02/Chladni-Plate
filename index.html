THE MECHANISM

How a flat plate learns to hold still

DRIVE

A single point pushes energy into the plate at a chosen frequency, Ω. Off-resonance, that energy barely raises a ripple — most of it never leaves the point it entered at.

DAMPING · ζ

Friction bleeds energy back out at every instant. Only the modes whose natural frequency sits close to Ω win that trade and build any real amplitude.

NODAL LINES

Everywhere the plate still moves, sand gets agitated and drifts down the amplitude gradient — until it runs out of slope, exactly on the lines that never move at all.

THE PHYSICS

A plate rings differently than a string

D∇⁴w + ρh w = f(x,y,t)

Every mode of a simply-supported square plate is a standing wave, sin(mπx) sin(nπy), with a natural frequency set by ωmn ∝ (m²+n²) — the biharmonic operator, not the ordinary wave equation. That's why a plate's overtones don't line up in the neat harmonic ratios a string's do.

Force the plate at frequency Ω and each mode behaves like its own damped oscillator. Sum enough of them, symmetrized around the plate's own geometry, and you get the exact field pushing sand toward the lines above.

Chladni Plate is a study of forced modal response on a simply-supported plate. No two plates ring quite the same.
